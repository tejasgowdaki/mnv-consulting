* **Company Vs. Limited Liability**

A **Company** structure is generally preferred when businesses plan to raise external investments, expand operations, or attract venture capital. It offers greater credibility but involves higher compliance and regulatory requirements

An **LLP,** on the other hand, is ideal for professional firms, consultants, and small to medium enterprises because it provides limited liability with lower compliance costs and operational flexibility.

| Basis | Company | Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Governing Law** | Governed by the Companies Act, 2013 | Governed by the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008 |
| **Legal Status** | A company is a separate legal entity distinct from its shareholders. | LLP is also a separate legal entity distinct from its partners. |
| **Liability** | Liability of shareholders is limited to the unpaid amount of shares held by them. | Liability of partners is limited to the agreed contribution in the LLP. |
| **Members / Owners** | Owners are called Shareholders or Members. | Owners are called Partners. |
| **Management** | Managed by a Board of Directors appointed by shareholders. | Managed by Designated Partners and Partners. |
| **Minimum   Members** | Minimum 2 members required for a Private Limited Company. | Minimum 2 partners required. |
| **Maximum Members** | Private Company: 200 members (excluding employees). | No maximum limit on partners. |
| **Compliance Requirements** | Higher compliance requirements such as board meetings, statutory audits, annual filings, and maintenance of statutory registers. | Lower compliance burden compared to companies. Fewer statutory meetings required. |
| **Audit Requirement** | Statutory audit is mandatory for every company irrespective of turnover. | Audit required only if turnover exceeds ₹40 lakhs or contribution exceeds ₹25 lakhs. |
| **Ownership Transfer** | Shares can be easily transferred, subject to restrictions in private companies. | Transfer of ownership requires amendment of LLP agreement and consent of partners. |
| **Fund Raising** | Easier to raise funds from investors, venture capitalists, or through share issuance. | LLP cannot issue shares, making external funding more difficult. |
| **Compliance Authority** | Regulated by the Registrar of Companies (ROC) under the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA). | Also regulated by the Registrar of Companies (ROC) under MCA. |
| **Perpetual Succession** | Company continues to exist even if shareholders change. | LLP also enjoys perpetual succession, independent of partners. |
| **Taxation** | Taxed as a separate corporate entity under the Income Tax Act. | LLP is taxed as a partnership firm under the Income Tax Act. |
